# VeLuny — Brand Guide
*Version 1.0 · July 2026*

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**VeLuny** is a baby parenting solutions brand. We start with sleep and soothing — our flagship product is a portable white noise sound machine that clips onto a stroller, crib, or diaper bag — but the brand is built to grow beyond it, into whatever practical problem tired parents run into next.

**Category today:** Baby sleep & soothing essentials
**Category long-term:** Baby & parenting solutions (multi-category)
**Flagship product:** Portable white noise / sound machine (soothing sounds, timer function, clip-on design, long battery life)
**Sales channel:** Shopify (DTC)

### Mission
To make parenting's hardest little moments easier to solve — starting with sleep, and expanding to wherever tired, overwhelmed parents need a smart, simple fix.

### Vision
VeLuny becomes a name parents trust across the parenting journey, not just for one product — the brand people think of when a new stage brings a new problem, because we solved the last one so well.

### The Problem We Solve
Parenting throws a constant stream of small, stressful problems — a baby who won't sleep away from home, a mess at feeding time, a meltdown mid-errand — and most brands only ever solve one of them. VeLuny starts with sleep and soothing, where the problem is losing a baby's sleep environment the moment you leave the house. A stroller walk, a grandparent's living room, a hotel room, the car — every new place threatens the nap. Our first product turns any location into a familiar, soothing one in seconds. As we grow, each new VeLuny product will follow the same logic: find a real parenting friction point, and make it disappear.

| **Category (brand ambition)** | Practical solutions across baby & parenting problems — sleep first, more to follow |

### Positioning Statement
*VeLuny makes practical solutions for the everyday problems of parenting. We started with sleep — a portable sound machine that brings a soothing, sleep-ready environment anywhere a family goes — because peace shouldn't be limited to the nursery. It's the first of many problems we intend to solve.*

### Why Start With Sleep
Sleep and soothing is the highest-frequency, highest-emotion pain point in early parenting, which makes it the right first category to build trust and word-of-mouth on. It also naturally introduces the brand's core mechanic — *"take a nursery-grade solution, make it portable"* — a pattern that can extend into feeding, bath time, travel, and beyond as VeLuny grows.

## 3. Brand Personality

VeLuny should feel like **a gentle, capable friend** — never clinical, never overly cutesy, never chaotic.

**We are:**
- Warm — soft-spoken, reassuring, human
- Playful — a little bounce and charm, never silly or childish
- Dependable — practical, no-nonsense when it comes to function
- Calm — nothing about the brand should feel loud, rushed, or overstimulating

**We are not:**
- Clinical or "medical device" cold
- Twee or saccharine ("baby talk" language, excessive cuteness)
- Trend-chasing or loud
- Overly minimalist to the point of feeling sterile

### Brand Archetype
**The Caregiver**, with a touch of **the Companion** — VeLuny nurtures, but it also travels alongside the family rather than sitting on a shelf waiting for them to come home.

## 4. Voice & Tone

**Voice traits:** Warm, plain-spoken, reassuring, lightly playful.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Naps that travel as well as you do." | "Revolutionary sleep technology for the modern infant." |
| "Clip it on, press play, breathe out." | "Our patented acoustic soothing algorithm..." |
| "For the walks, the waiting rooms, the wherever." | "OMG your bebe will LOVE this!!! 😴💤" |
| Speak to the *parent's* relief, not just the baby's sleep | Don't diagnose, over-promise medical outcomes, or use fear-based marketing ("Is your baby's development at risk?") |

**Tone shifts by context:**
- **Product page:** Confident, benefit-led, practical (specs matter to this buyer)
- **Social captions:** Warmer, funnier, more relatable to parent exhaustion/humor
- **Packaging & unboxing:** Quiet, reassuring, a little celebratory (this is often a gift)
- **Customer support:** Patient, human, zero jargon

## 5. Logo

VeLuny's logotype is a custom, hand-lettered script-serif hybrid — bold, bouncy strokes with rounded terminals and a confident forward lean. It reads as friendly but never childish, thanks to its weight and warmth-through-craft rather than through decoration.

### Logo Variants
- **Black wordmark** — `VeLuny.` in ink black, for use on light/cream backgrounds
- **White (cream) wordmark** — `VeLuny.` in warm off-white, for use on dark backgrounds
- **Icon** — the standalone lowercase "v." mark, for favicons, app icons, social avatars, and small-space applications (packaging seals, stickers)

### The Period
The full stop after "VeLuny" is a permanent part of the mark — a small, confident punctuation that reads as "settled" and "done," reinforcing the brand's promise of resolution (baby is asleep, full stop). Never remove it.

### Clear Space
Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the lowercase "e" in the wordmark, on all sides. Nothing else — text, imagery, or edges — should enter that zone.

### Minimum Size
- Wordmark: no smaller than 100px wide (digital) / 1 inch wide (print)
- Icon alone: no smaller than 32px (digital) / 0.25 inch (print)

### Logo Don'ts
- Don't recolor the wordmark outside the approved palette
- Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the mark
- Don't add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Don't place the black wordmark on busy photography or low-contrast backgrounds
- Don't separate the icon's "v" and period, or alter their proportions
- Don't recreate the wordmark in a different typeface

## 6. Color Palette

The current identity is intentionally two-tone — ink and cream — which keeps the logo feeling premium and legible. For broader brand use (packaging, web UI, social), we extend it with a small set of soft, nursery-appropriate supporting colors.

### Core (from the logo)

| Name | Hex | RGB | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **VeLuny Ink** | `#222222` | 34, 34, 40 | Primary wordmark color, body text, primary buttons |
| **VeLuny Cream** | `#F5F4F0` | 245, 244, 240 | Primary background, reversed wordmark color |
| **Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Secondary background, packaging white space |

### Suggested Supporting Palette
*(For UI accents, packaging color-coding by sound/scent variant, badges, and illustration — to be sampled against real packaging/photography before finalizing)*

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Dusty Sky** | `#A9C2CE` | Calm accent — buttons, icons, soft backgrounds |
| **Sage Whisper** | `#B7C4A8` | Secondary accent — nature/organic sound categories |
| **Blush Nap** | `#E8C9C3` | Warm accent — gifting, registry, packaging ribbon/tape |
| **Warm Sand** | `#D8C9B0` | Neutral middle tone between Ink and Cream, for cards/sections |

**Usage rule of thumb:** Ink + Cream carry the brand. Accent colors should appear in small doses — never compete with the logo, and never all appear together in one layout.

## 7. Typography

### Logo Typeface
The wordmark is bespoke lettering — not a licensed font — so it should never be recreated in text. Treat it strictly as a logo asset.

### Recommended Brand Typefaces
To echo the logo's warmth and rounded, confident character in everyday typography (web, packaging copy, emails):

| Role | Typeface | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Display / Headlines** | Fraunces (or similar warm, low-contrast serif) | Echoes the logo's soft, rounded terminals and gives headlines a little handcrafted warmth |
| **Body / UI text** | Quicksand or Nunito (rounded sans-serif) | Rounded letterforms tie back to the logo's soft curves while staying highly legible at small sizes |
| **Fallback system stack** | `Georgia, "Times New Roman", serif` (display) / `-apple-system, "Segoe UI", sans-serif` (body) | For email clients and platforms without custom font support |

**Hierarchy example:**
- H1: Fraunces, 40–56px, Ink on Cream
- H2/H3: Fraunces or Quicksand Bold, 24–32px
- Body: Quicksand or Nunito Regular, 16–18px, generous line height (1.5–1.6) for a calm, unhurried reading feel

## 8. Imagery & Photography Style

**Mood:** Soft, natural light. Real homes, real strollers, real tired-but-happy parents — not overly staged studio shots.

**Guidelines:**
- Favor warm, slightly desaturated tones that sit comfortably next to the Cream/Ink palette — avoid cool, clinical white studio lighting
- Show the product *in use and in motion*: clipped to a stroller mid-walk, tucked into a diaper bag, on a car seat — reinforcing portability
- Include genuine parent-and-baby moments over posed "product hero" shots when possible
- Avoid high-contrast, high-saturation, or "tech gadget" style photography — this is a soothing product, not a piece of consumer electronics
- Night/dim lighting shots are welcome and on-brand (nightlight feature, bedtime context) — keep them warm-toned, not blue-toned

**Avoid:**
- Stock-photo perfection (overly polished, artificial-looking families)
- Clinical, sterile, or hospital-like settings
- Loud or cluttered backgrounds that compete with the product

## 9. Iconography & Graphic Elements

- Use simple, rounded-line icons (matching the logo's soft terminals) for feature call-outs on packaging and the product page — e.g., sound waves, a clock (timer), a battery, a stroller clip
- The **"v."** icon mark can be used as a repeating pattern element (packaging tissue, stickers, social highlight covers) at low opacity in Cream-on-Ink or Ink-on-Cream
- Sound waves/soft wavy lines make a natural secondary motif, reinforcing "sound machine" without needing literal imagery

## 10. Applications

### Shopify Store
- Background: Cream (`#F5F4F0`) as the dominant site background, not stark white — keeps the calm, soft feeling consistent with the logo
- Primary buttons: Ink (`#222222`) fill, Cream text
- Use Dusty Sky or Blush Nap sparingly for secondary buttons, sale badges, or trust icons (free shipping, warranty, etc.)
- Product photography on Cream or neutral home backgrounds, not pure white e-commerce backgrounds, to keep the "soothing" feeling even in the grid view

### Packaging
- Black logo on Cream box stock, or Cream (reversed) logo on an Ink-colored box — pick one primary direction for consistency
- Consider Blush Nap as a ribbon/sticker accent color for the "gift-ready" registry angle

### Social Media
- Profile icon: the "v." mark, Ink on Cream
- Grid consistency: warm-toned lifestyle photography, occasional Ink/Cream text-based posts (tips, testimonials) using Fraunces headlines
- Caption tone: warmer and more human than the product page — lean into parent humor and relatable exhaustion, always landing on reassurance

## 11. Brand Architecture — Built to Scale Beyond Sleep

VeLuny is designed as a **house brand**, not a single-product brand: one identity, one voice, one look — applied across multiple parenting problem-areas over time. This section exists so future product lines stay recognizably "VeLuny" without needing a new logo, palette, or tone every time.

### What stays fixed across every category
- The `VeLuny.` wordmark, icon, and full color system (Section 6) — never a sub-logo or category-specific mark
- The voice (Section 4) — warm, plain-spoken, reassuring, regardless of whether the product is a sound machine or a bath-time tool
- The core brand mechanic: **identify one real, high-frequency parenting frustration, and solve it simply and portably.** Every product should be able to finish the sentence *"VeLuny makes ___ easier."*

### What can flex by category
- **Sub-line naming:** Group future products by the problem they solve rather than by product type, e.g. *VeLuny Sleep* (sound machines, nightlights), *VeLuny Feed*, *VeLuny Travel* — simple, plain-English descriptors, not invented sub-brand names that compete with VeLuny itself
- **Accent color per sub-line:** Use the supporting palette (Section 6) to quietly color-code categories on packaging and the site — e.g., Dusty Sky for Sleep, Blush Nap for Feed — while Ink and Cream remain constant across all of them
- **Photography subject matter:** Naturally shifts with the product (bath time vs. stroller walks) but should always follow the same mood and lighting direction in Section 8

### Guardrails as you expand
- Don't launch a category the brand voice can't comfortably talk about in the same reassuring, non-clinical tone (e.g., avoid drifting into medical-device territory)
- Don't let a new product line's marketing outweigh or contradict the "calm, practical, portable" promise established by the sound machine
- Before adding a new category, ask: *does this solve a real, frequent parenting frustration, the way the sound machine solves "lost sleep environment"?* If a product is just "another baby gadget," it's off-brand regardless of category

### Homepage & Store Implication
Even while VeLuny sells only the sound machine, the Shopify store's language should leave room to grow — e.g., "VeLuny's first solution: sleep that travels with you" rather than "VeLuny, the sleep machine company." This keeps today's copy honest while setting up tomorrow's expansion without needing a re-launch or rebrand.
